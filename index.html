The Framework

PBIS is how we
teach behavior —
the same way we
teach academics.

Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) is Georgia's evidence-based behavioral framework, defined in state law as part of Georgia's MTSS model. Vidalia City Schools implements PBIS as the behavioral component of Georgia's MTSS framework.

We teach expectations. We acknowledge students who meet them. We respond instructionally when they don't. And we use data — not instinct — to guide every decision.

See the three-tier model

Outcomes

Valued goals for students, families, and educators that drive every decision.

Data

Information that identifies needs, guides practice selection, and measures results.

Practices

Evidence-based interventions matched precisely to the level of student need.

Systems

Coaching, PD, leadership, and teaming structures that sustain implementation.

Georgia's PBIS Model

Three tiers. Every student.

The tiers are cumulative — not sequential. Every student receives Tier 1. Additional tiers layer on top, never replace what came before.

Tier 1
Universal
80%
of students

School-wide and classroom expectations, proactive management, and positive acknowledgment for every student, every day.

  • →Teaching matrix and expectations
  • →The 5 essential classroom practices
  • →Behavior-specific praise & reward systems
  • →Active supervision in all settings

Tier 2
Targeted
15%
of students

Small-group, structured interventions for students who need more than Tier 1 — data-driven, time-limited, and monitored.

  • →Check-in / Check-out (CICO)
  • →Behavior contracts
  • →Social skills groups
  • →Structured mentoring (2×10 strategy)
Hover or tap to expand ↓
Tier 3
Intensive
5%
of students

Highly individualized supports for students with complex, chronic needs — built on a functional understanding of behavior.

  • →Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA)
  • →Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP)
  • →Wraparound community supports
  • →Intensive progress monitoring
Hover or tap to expand ↓

Tiers are cumulative, not sequential. A student receiving Tier 3 support also receives Tier 2 and Tier 1 simultaneously. Adding a tier means layering support on — never removing what came before.
